Part 1: Defining + Graphing Quadratic Functions Quadratic Function A function in which, f(x)=ax²+bx+c, where a, b, and c are numbers with a ≠ 0 Graphical Quadratic Function The graph of a quadratic has a "U-shape" that either opens up (positive) or down (negative) Properties of a [Parabolic Curve](obsidian://open?vault=Vault&file=Index%2F10-19%20Course%20Notes%2F11%20-%20STATS%2F11-2%2FKey%20Points%2F2.2%2FParabolic%20Curve)

    - The sign of ```a``` and the coefficient of ```x²```, determine the direction in which the [parabola](obsidian://open?vault=Vault&file=Index%2F10-19%20Course%20Notes%2F11%20-%20STATS%2F11-2%2FKey%20Points%2F2.2%2FParabolic%20Curve) will open
    - When ```a < 0 ```, the parabola opens down
    *Vertex*
        When a [parabola](obsidian://open?vault=Vault&file=Index%2F10-19%20Course%20Notes%2F11%20-%20STATS%2F11-2%2FKey%20Points%2F2.2%2FParabolic%20Curve) opens **down**, the y-value of the vertex is the **maximum**
        When a [parabola](obsidian://open?vault=Vault&file=Index%2F10-19%20Course%20Notes%2F11%20-%20STATS%2F11-2%2FKey%20Points%2F2.2%2FParabolic%20Curve) opens **up**, the y-value of the vertex is the **minimum**
    *[[Finding the Vertex]]*
        - Vertex x-coordinates  = ```-b/2a```
        - Vertex y-coordinates = ```f(-b/2a)```
        - Axis of Symmetry = ```same as vertex x-coordinate```
        - Zeros ([[X-intercepts]]) = ```y=0```
            Locations in which the [parabola](obsidian://open?vault=Vault&file=Index%2F10-19%20Course%20Notes%2F11%20-%20STATS%2F11-2%2FKey%20Points%2F2.2%2FParabolic%20Curve) crosses the x-axis
