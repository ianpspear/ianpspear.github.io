Part 1: Polynomials

Monomial

a single termed polynomial

  • y=7x
  • y=4
  • f(x)=-9x⁴

Binomial

expressions with two terms that are separated by an addition (+) or subtraction (-) sign

  • y=4-9x
  • y=x²+1
  • f(x)=x³+7x

Trinomial

three terms separated by addition (+) and/or subtraction (-) signs

  • y=3x⁵-6x²+7

Polynomial Standard Form

Coefficients: aₙ,aₙ(sub -1),...,a₁,a₀, in which a≠0, otherwise the term aₙxⁿ, would be zero

The leading coefficient in Standard Polynomial Form is the coefficient of the term with the largest exponent


Degree

When working with polynomials the location in which the graph crosses the x-axis


Turning Point

A place where the graph changes from going up to going down or vice-versa

  • When the degree of the polynomial is even, both ends of the graph will go in the same direction
  • When the degree of the polynomial is odd, both ends of the graph will go in different directions
  • The degree of the polynomial is usually, at least one more than the turning points
