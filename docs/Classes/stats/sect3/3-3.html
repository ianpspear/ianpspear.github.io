Part 1: Average Rates of Change

Average Rate of Change

The average rate of change of f(x) with respect to x for a function f as x changes from a to b is (f(b) - f(a))/(b - a)

Part 2: Instantaneous Rates of Change

Instantaneous Rate of Change

The instantaneous rate of change for a function f at x = a is lim (h→0) (f(a + h) - f(a))/h provided this limit exists.

Alternate Form: Let b = a + h. The instantaneous rate of change for a function f when x = a can be written as lim (b→a) (f(b) - f(a))/(b - a) provided this limit exists.

Business Applications of Instantaneous Rate of Change

Marginal Analysis

  • Marginal Cost: The instantaneous rate of change of the cost function (or the approximate cost of producing one additional item).
  • Marginal Revenue: The instantaneous rate of change of the revenue function (or the approximate revenue from selling one additional item).
  • Marginal Profit: The instantaneous rate of change of the profit function (or the approximate profit from producing and selling one additional item).
