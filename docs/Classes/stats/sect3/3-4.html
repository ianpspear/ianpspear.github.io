Section 3.4 - Derivatives

The Definition of a Derivative

  • Secant Line
    • A secant line passes through two points on a curve.
  • Tangent Line
    • A tangent line to a curve at one point 𝑃 touches the curve at 𝑃, but not at any other points nearby.
    • A tangent line at a point P indicates the direction of travel as you travel along the function and pass through point 𝑃.

Slopes of Secant and Tangent Lines

  • Slope of a Secant Line
    • The form of the slope of a Secant Line is: (𝑓(𝑎+ℎ)−𝑓(𝑎))/((𝑎+ℎ)−𝑎)
    • Represents Average rate of change
  • Slope of a Tangent Line
    • Represents the Instantaneous Rate of Change
