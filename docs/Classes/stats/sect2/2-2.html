Part 1: Defining + Graphing Quadratic Functions

Quadratic Function

Quadratic Function:
f(x) = ax² + bx + c
where a, b, and c are numbers with a ≠ 0

Graphical Quadratic Function

Graphical Quadratic Function:
The graph of a quadratic has a "U-shape" that either opens up (positive) or down (negative)

Properties of a Parabolic Curve

Properties of a Parabolic Curve:
- The sign of a and the coefficient of x² determine the direction in which the parabola will open
- When a < 0, the parabola opens down
Vertex
- When a parabola opens down, the y-value of the vertex is the maximum
- When a parabola opens up, the y-value of the vertex is the minimum
Finding the Vertex
  1. Vertex x-coordinates = -b/2a
  2. Vertex y-coordinates = f(-b/2a)
  3. Axis of Symmetry = same as vertex x-coordinate
  4. Zeros (X-intercepts) = y=0 - Locations in which the parabola crosses the x-axis

Part 2: Solving Quadratic Functions

Three Methods to Solve:

  1. Factor
  2. Quadratic Formula
  3. Complete the Square

A quadratic will have no X-intercepts if the radicand is negative.
